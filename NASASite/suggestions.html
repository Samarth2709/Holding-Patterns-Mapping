Suggestions

Suggestion 1:

In general, holding patterns allow separation between aircraft. Speed restrictions prevent aircraft from altering speed to create separation. We propose allowing for exceptions of speed restrictions when separation is needed to lessen the need for holding patterns significantly at many major airports. Enabling aircraft to slow down prior to arrival could lessen the need for holding patterns, decreasing circling time and carbon emissions.

Suggestion 2:

Our second proposal to reduce holding patterns is to utilize runway switches. When arrival routes are clogged, one or more aircrafts are usually put into a holding pattern. While harder to execute, all factors should be considered. Both solutions come with challenges. Speed restrictions are in place for a reason. By allowing for exceptions, the complexity of arrivals and departures would increase significantly. The second solution depends on the availability of free runways. When operating at max, airports may not have runways to spare. By moving an aircraft to another runway, more issues may emerge, resulting in more traffic.
